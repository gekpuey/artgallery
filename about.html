About Us

A small gallery, run at a slower pace.

Linden House opened in 2011 in a converted terrace house on Aldergate Row. We represent six painters, show new work twice a year, and try to know the story behind every piece we hang.

Our story

Linden House began as a single room above a framer's workshop, where founder Alice Kerrigan started showing paintings by friends from art school who couldn't get gallery representation elsewhere. Fifteen years on, we've kept the same instinct: work first, everything else second.

We don't run a stable of forty artists or chase art-fair trends. We work closely with six painters, most of whom we've represented for over five years, and we spend real time in their studios before anything goes on our walls. When you buy a piece from Linden House, we can usually tell you exactly when and why it was made.

How we choose

We look for painters who return to the same subject repeatedly rather than chasing variety — a marsh, a coastline, a single sitter. That kind of sustained attention tends to produce work that holds up on a wall for years, not just in a first glance.

Every artist we represent works in traditional media: oil, acrylic, watercolor, gouache, charcoal or mixed media on paper or canvas. No prints, no editions, no digital reproductions — what you see in the gallery or on this site is the only version of that piece that will ever exist.
