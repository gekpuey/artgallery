How to Sell

Selling through Linden House.

We take on new artists rarely and deliberately. If you paint in oil, acrylic, watercolor, gouache, charcoal or mixed media and want to be considered, here's how the process works.

The process

Send a submission

Email 8–12 images of recent work, a short note on your practice, and a link to your website or Instagram if you have one, to submissions@lindenhousegallery.example. We do not accept physical submissions or portfolio drop-offs.

Studio visit

If your work fits our current programme, we'll arrange a studio visit. This is the most important step — we want to see unfinished work and understand how you work, not just the finished pieces.

Consignment agreement

Work is taken on consignment, not purchased outright. We agree a retail price together, and Linden House takes a 50% commission on sale — standard for a gallery at our size. Unsold work can be withdrawn at any point with 30 days' notice.

Showing your work

New consignments typically join the year-round collection first. Artists who continue to place well are considered for a dedicated exhibition slot — see our exhibition programme.

What we're looking for

We respond best to painters with a sustained, recognisable body of work — a consistent subject, palette or process across many pieces, rather than a portfolio of one-off experiments. We currently show landscape, portrait, abstract, seascape, urban and botanical subjects, but we're open to strong work outside those categories too.

Because we represent a small number of artists closely, we can't guarantee a response time, but every submission is reviewed by our team.
